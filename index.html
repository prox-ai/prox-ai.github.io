Personalized AI-Driven Virtual Try-On

AI Team, PROXGLOBAL

Abstract

Virtual try-on technologies are being rigorously evaluated using the latest models and actual user data to assess performance and provide insights. This paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of contemporary virtual try-on solutions, identifying common challenges and limitations in generating accurate and realistic garment fittings on diverse body types. To enhance user experience across various scenarios, a new pipeline is proposed that integrates multiple models. This multi-model approach aims to improve the fidelity and adaptability of virtual try-ons, ensuring more personalized and realistic results. This research not only provides a critical analysis of existing systems but also advances the field by suggesting a scalable and versatile framework capable of handling complex scenarios in virtual garment fitting.

Proposed Method

Fashion Segmentation
The first step involves segmenting the clothing item from its background. This is essential for cleanly isolating the garment to be tried on, ensuring that only the clothing item is processed in subsequent steps.

Human Segmentation
This step involves segmenting the human figure from the background of the input image. Accurate segmentation is crucial for properly overlaying the clothing onto the human figure.

Keypoint Extraction
Key anatomical points on the human body are identified, such as joints and the outlines of the body. These keypoints help in aligning the clothing item with the person’s body structure, ensuring that the garment fits correctly in terms of orientation and scale.

Try On Diffusion
Utilizing a diffusion model, the segmented clothing item is intelligently mapped onto the segmented human figure, considering the keypoints to adapt the clothing to the pose and proportions of the person. This model is responsible for handling the complex deformations of the garment.

Faceswap
In this optional step, the face of the person in the try-on image can be swapped with another face. This could be used for privacy reasons or to allow users to see how the outfit looks on different people.

Image Generation
This step can either generate a new image of a person based on specified attributes like gender, age, height, and body width, or enhance the realism of the composited image in the try-on application.

The diagram also depicts an integrated flow where user inputs, such as personal dimensions and clothing choices, are processed through these technological steps to produce a realistic simulation of how different outfits would look on them. This method is particularly advantageous for online shopping, allowing users to preview outfits virtually, which can enhance user experience and potentially increase sales for retailers.

Various Try-On: Research and Results



Outfit Anyone

HR-VTON (High-Resolution Virtual Try-On Network) represents a significant advancement in the VTON field by prioritizing high-resolution image generation to ensure the visual quality and fidelity of the output. The core innovation of HR-VTON lies in its multi-stage architecture, which progressively refines the quality of synthesized images. This methodology incorporates several stages, including a coarse-level garment warping phase and a fine-level detail enhancement phase.

The garment warping phase employs a geometry-aware module that aligns the clothing with the target body's pose and shape, ensuring that the initial placement of the garment is both realistic and contextually appropriate. This stage leverages advanced techniques in geometric transformation to minimize distortions and maintain the integrity of garment features.
